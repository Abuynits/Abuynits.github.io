Behavior Cloning (BC) enables robots to learn
from expert demonstrations, but generating sufficient data
from human operators can be prohibitively expensive and
time-consuming. Using classical motion planners as experts
provides a more cost-efficient and time-effective alternative,
however, these approaches still face significant bottlenecks as
data generation remains computationally expensive to generate
high-quality paths. In this paper, we demonstrate the potential
of hardware-accelerated motion planning to accelerate BC
pipelines that use classical planners as experts.
